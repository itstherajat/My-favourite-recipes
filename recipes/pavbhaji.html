Pav Bhaji

Ingredients of Pav Bhaji

    Ingredients

  • 2 potato
  • 1/2 cup capsicum
  • 1/2 cup carrot
  • 1/4 cup pumpkin
  • 2 cup water
  • 2 teaspoon refined oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • 2 tablespoon pav bhaji masala
  • 2 tablespoon pav bhaji masala
  • 2 teaspoon coriander leaves
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup cauliflower
  • 1/2 cup cabbage
  • 1/4 cup green beans
  • 1/4 cup peas
  • salt as required
  • 4 tablespoon butter
  • 1/2 cup onion
  • 1 teaspoon deggi chilli
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/4 cup veg stock
  • 2 teaspoon kasoori methi leaves
  • 10 pav

How to make Pav Bhaji

    Steps needed for making the dish

  1. Boil the potatoes for 10 minutes until they are soft and mashable. Remove from flame and peel and mash the potatoes. Roughly chop or shred all the other vegetables in a pan with 2 cups of water and a little salt. Boil the veggies now. Once done, remove from flame and allow them to cool. Do not throw away the water as it has a lot of nutrition and can be used as the veg stock.
  2. Put a pan over medium flame and heat oil and 1 tablespoon butter together. Add cumin seeds in it and then add onions. Saute for a minute. Then, add 1 tsp garlic water to it (finely chopped garlic in 1 tbsp water). Now, add all the masala - coriander powder, deggi mirch, cumin powder, turmeric and salt to taste. Cook for a minute and then add tomato puree to it. Cook for a minute and then add mashed potatoes to it. Now, add the remaining veg stock of boiled veggies. Cook for 1-2 minutes and then add pav bhaji masala, kasoori methi, and coriander leaves. Stir well and then add 4 tbsp butter to the bhaji. Mix well and let it cook for another 5-10 minutes. Use a pav bhaji masher if possible to mash it further. Once done your bhaji is ready
  3. Now, put a pan over medium flame and roast pav in it. Once done, serve them with Bhaji. Garnish with some chopped onion and top the bhaji with some butter and coriander leaves.
